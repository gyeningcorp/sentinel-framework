# The Sentinel Framework
### A Standard for Agentic AI Governance

> *"Don't give AI a job. Give it a task. Know every token it spends. Put a human in the middle — not at the end."*
> — Christopher Gyening, 2026

---

## What Is This?

The Sentinel Framework is an open governance standard for organizations deploying AI agents. It defines five pillars of safe, accountable, and auditable agentic AI — moving from vague "AI safety" concepts to concrete, implementable architecture.

This is not a product. It is a standard — like OWASP for web security, or RFC for internet protocols.

## The Five Pillars

| # | Pillar | One Line |
|---|--------|----------|
| 1 | [Token Accountability](docs/01-token-accountability.md) | Every token is a decision. Audit the decisions. |
| 2 | [Human-In-The-Middle (HITM)](docs/02-human-in-the-middle.md) | Humans don't just approve. They steer. |
| 3 | [RAG vs. LAG](docs/03-rag-lag.md) | Memory for truth. Logs for drift. |
| 4 | [Least Privilege Agency](docs/04-least-privilege-agency.md) | AI gets a badge, not a master key. |
| 5 | [Tasks Over Jobs](docs/05-tasks-over-jobs.md) | Atomic actions. Not open mandates. |
| 6 | [Network Security Architecture](docs/06-network-security.md) | Where your AI lives determines what it can touch. |

## Who Is This For?

- **Executives** deploying AI in their organizations
- **Engineers** building agentic systems
- **Compliance & Legal** teams governing AI use
- **Researchers** studying AI safety in production
